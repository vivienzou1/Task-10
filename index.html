From: Ajit Singh
Director, eBusiness Consulting
Subject: Improving Carnegie Financial Services Web site's search visibility

Team,

We have recently been retained by Carnegie Financial Services (CFS), a company aspiring to be a leader in the financial service industry, who have a problem with their Web site. CEO Dara Griffith did a Web search related to their flagship products, and the site didn’t show up in any of her searches. To make matters worse, she found that their competitors’ sites were high on her list of search results. CFS is seeking our help to fix this problem immediately!!!

As you know, having an effective Web presence compatible with the company strategy is critical. CFS's current strategy is to help their online customers find their better-known, flagship products quickly and easily.

To do this, I'll need you to first run Web searches using their major product lines and names as your key search words (attached is a document with Dara’s phrases with some notes from me). If you run these searches now, only CFS's competitors will appear in the top ten search results. Next, create a list of their competitors that have the best Web presence. Base your list on how consistently each site ranks within the top ten search results. From this list of successful sites, determine and analyze the specific characteristics that make them effective and highly visible. These are the sites to beat with respect to ranking on Web searches for their flagship products. Finally, implement changes to the CFS Web site based on your analysis of these characteristics. CFS's ultimate goal is to consistently appear in the top ten results of a search related to their flagship products.

To help you do these activities, I have attached the current CFS Web site and related files. I have also attached some templates to use as you make your changes. I have arranged for a search engine that you can use on your machine, and a list of URL’s of what can be called as CFS's web presence competitors. Both these resources will be provided to you separately.

Before taking the improved site online, however, we will need to convince a panel of CFS executives that changes we suggest are valid. And to ensure high visibility of future Web sites, we have to write a “handbook” of recommendations that will be referenced by their web development teams. Towards these objectives, I'll need you to document your efforts as outlined below.

Please submit to me:

  1. An analysis report containing:
    • A summary of Carnegie Financial Services’ business objectives & target audience.
    • A list of potential search terms, keywords, and phrases that their customers are likely to use.
    • Site analysis of their top 5 competitors (from the search results).
    • Suggested attractor and discriminatory terms to be used on the CFS site.
  2. A site redesign report containing:
    • Snapshot of rankings of CFS’s Web site before modifications are implemented.
    • Snapshot of rankings of CFS’s Web site after modifications are implemented.
    • “Changes made” section which documents the reasons for all major changes and redesigns.
    • New HTML site.
  3. A design recommendation report.
    • How to go about Identifying and incorporating key terms.
    • How to Implement a good design.
    • Specific do’s and don’ts.

I know that you will do great work on this mission-critical project! Good luck!

Regards,

Ajit Singh
